01 The Concept

Gizmo is a bullet-heaven / survivors-like with an unusually deep "juice" layer. The gameplay is proven and fun. This bible re-skins the placeholder art into one cohesive, premium, dopamine-forward identity.

The World — "The Hush"

A vast, dormant machine-cosmos drained of color. When Shape Storms roll in — waves of charged geometric debris — the dark briefly comes alive. The player's job is to ride the storm and light it up.

The Hero — "Gizmo"

A tiny, plucky spark-bot: a glowing cyan core in a friendly chassis. Reads as a comma of light even at 16px. Gizmo sweeps up loose Sparks, cracks Caches, and discharges brilliant Novas.

The fiction IS the design philosophy

A disciplined dark, minimal void where light = reward. Every dopamine burst is earned and distilled against calm negative space. The world justifies the aesthetic: you are literally bringing color back to the dark.

02 Design Pillars

1 · Distilled dopamine

Calm, readable core gameplay. Bright, loud payoffs. The quieter the field, the harder a Nova or Cache-crack hits. Minimalism makes the pop land.

2 · Juice is the design

Feedback isn't decoration — it's the product. Screen-shake, squash, particles, escalating numbers and audio-synced pulses are first-class, not afterthoughts.

3 · Confident, friendly geometry

Flat vector forms, rounded but never babyish, with bold outlines and soft inner glow. Premium-mobile restraint (Alto / Monument Valley) on a neon-dark field (Geometry Wars).

4 · Every shape is alive

Enemies have faces and attitude. This is the anti-generic move: the "storm" has personality, so the world feels handcrafted, not asset-flipped.

5 · Read it in a glance — at couch distance

Mixed ages, busy screens. The HUD must stay legible against the loudest moment. Color-code every system; surface meters only when they matter (Hades / Dead Space contextual HUD).

Usage rules

  • Enemies are cool & matte, low-glow. They are the storm/dark. Indigo, dusty coral, charcoal. This keeps the field calm.
  • Pickups & the player are bright & high-glow. Sparks, Caches, Gizmo's core — these own the light, so they read instantly as "good / get this."
  • Each system keeps its hue everywhere — meter, icon, callout, particle, and the upgrade that feeds it. Color is the player's instant value-read.
  • Gold is sacred. Reserve it for Surge, Caches, score and "best/evolve." Don't dilute it on chrome.

Scale & rules

  • Hero numbers use Fredoka with a tonal gradient + glow; they should scale up and settle on change (juice).
  • Eyebrows / labels: Nunito 900, uppercase, letter-spacing 1.5–2px, dimmed. Never the star — they frame the value.
  • Minimum 11px. Tabular figures for anything that ticks. Sentence case for body, ALL-CAPS only for short callouts & eyebrows.

05 Form Language

Construction

  • Built from circles, rounded triangles, rounded squares, hexagons, diamonds.
  • Generous corner radii — friendly, stable, readable small.
  • Confident outline (cool dark, ~6px at 200px) for cel-style definition.
  • Soft outer glow only on energized things; matte for inert.

Light & life

  • Single soft top light → subtle top sheen, cool underside.
  • One bright focal core per hero element (Gizmo's lens, a Cache core).
  • Enemies get faces — eyes + brow set their attitude.
  • Flat fills with a single tonal gradient; no rendered realism.

09 Motion, Juice & VFX

The most important section. Borrowed from Balatro and the Vlambeer "game-feel" school: feedback is layered, high-density, and synced.

Always-on game feel

  • Squash & stretch on Gizmo's thrust, pickups popping, enemies dying.
  • Screen-shake scaled to event weight — a tap on a Spark, a thump on a Cache-crack.
  • Particles in the system's hue: spark trails, Cache confetti, Nova rings.
  • Hit-stop (1–3 frame freeze) on big pops to sell impact.

The big beats

  • Level-up Nova: field dims a beat, then a hue-shifting shockwave relights it. The minimalism → payoff moment.
  • Cache crack: gold flash, light beam, reward arcs out, chunky number.
  • Economy bursts: each (Flow/Clutch/Echo/Surge) detonates in its own color so the player knows which joy just fired.
Audio-visual sync — the Balatro lesson

Numbers escalate in pitch and size together. A scoring chain should feel like a slot-machine cascade: each tick lands a sound, a scale-bump, and a shake in lockstep. The dual channel is what turns arithmetic into a jackpot.

Accessibility  Honor prefers-reduced-motion: keep color/scale feedback, drop shake and heavy particles. Never rely on color alone — pair every hue with an icon and motion.

10 Alternate Directions

The primary above is Lumen & Static: characterful but disciplined. Two dials on either side, included so claude.ai/design can explore the edges.

A · Paper Tinkerer warmer

Elevate the original hand-painted/watercolor "inventor" world into a cohesive, premium storybook craft look. Warm paper, ink lines, gouache texture. Lower contrast, cozier, more handmade. Best if you want charm over arcade punch.

B · Pure Neon Vector cooler

Strip the faces: Gizmo becomes a pure glyph of light, enemies become clean signed shapes. Maximal Geometry-Wars minimalism and arcade purity. Best if you want the most abstract, "distilled" reading of Gizmo.

11 References — the blend

  • Balatro — juice is the design; audio-synced escalation; minimalism that detonates.
  • Geometry Wars — neon-on-dark, glowing geometric storms. The genre's native look.
  • Vampire Survivors / 20 Minutes Till Dawn / Brotato — survivors-like density, build & level-up loops.
  • Alto's Odyssey / Monument Valley — flat-vector premium restraint; "each frame worthy of display."
  • Hades — striking yet non-intrusive HUD under heavy action.
  • Astro Bot — premium, lovable robot mascot; expression through a strong, simple silhouette.
