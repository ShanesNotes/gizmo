# The Lumen Codex — Gizmo × Symbolic World (Fusion, v1.0)

*Where ancient symbolism meets futuristic arcade. A spark re-illuminates a sleeping codex — and the dopamine becomes a verdict.*

Portable companion to `Fusion-Codex.html`. This layers the **Symbolic World** grammar onto the **"Lumen & Static"** direction.

---

## 1. Two worlds meet

**Lumen & Static** gave Gizmo a premium neon-on-dark identity. **Symbolic World** gives it meaning that *changes state* — its grammar reads sacred/illuminated art (gold-ground, sealed emblems, charged empty space, borders that behave, materials that remember) as game design.

They look like opposites: the arcade runs on glow, sparkle, escalation, speed; the codex on ink, gold-leaf, restraint, weight. **The hinge is gold.** Surge — the arcade's biggest payoff — is gold. Illumination — the manuscript's word for blessing made visible in gold-leaf — is also gold. So the neon *light* sits inside the ancient *gold-ground*, and every payoff becomes an **illumination**.

**New world — "The Hush":** a vast, dormant **illuminated codex** with its pages gone dark. **Gizmo** is the spark that wakes the marks; surviving the Shape Storm re-illuminates the page.

## 2. The one law we honor

> "A symbol is game-ready only when it changes state." — Symbolic World

No bolted-on decoration. Every borrowed mark must carry a verdict, hold a covenant, seal a reward, or remember a wound. The corpus's **drift-to-reject** even names arcade habits — *no rarity glow, no victory sparkle, no green-smoke evil, no modern HUD hiding state.* We don't ignore these; we **negotiate** them (§7), keeping kid-friendly joy while grounding it in matter.

## 4. The four covenants

The signature economies become four **covenants**, each an illuminated roundel (ink + gold seal, stipple radiance, neon-hue light within). The meter *is* the covenant's state. Flow·Thread (mint) · Clutch·Breath (cyan) · Echo·Vigil (violet) · Surge·Seal (gold). See `assets-fusion/covenant-emblems.svg`.

## 6. Type & technique

**Three-part type:** **Fredoka** (arcade display + dopamine numbers; now gold-leafed with ink stroke = inscription) · **Cormorant Garamond** (the manuscript voice — labels, verdicts, taglines) · **Nunito** (clean UI body).

**Material technique:**
- **Stipple radiance** — glow built from dots/marks, not only blur. Radiance is a *texture*.
- **Ink contour + gold-leaf rule-lines** on panels; **corner wax-seals**.
- **Charged empty space** frames the payoff — quiet makes the illumination land.
- **Material memory** — craquelure & seams; restoration keeps the scar.
- The **aureole** (gold-ground energy roundel) is geometric & secular — never a literal sacred halo.

## 7. Fidelity — respecting the Symbolic World

**We keep:** *Emulate the hand, never copy the page* (source execution at weight 1.0, literal copying at 0.0) · translate motifs into operations (no literal apples/towers/hair) · **no sacred copying** (no saints, clergy, liturgical text, religious halos) · emblem verdicts not badges · state before label.

**The negotiated tension** (corpus rejects "rarity glow" / "victory sparkle"; the kids' arcade needs joy):
- Rarity signals by **gold-leaf & seal density** first, glow second.
- Sparkle is allowed but **resolves into an illumination** (a verdict), never empty confetti.
- Glow co-exists with **stipple radiance** so light reads as *made*.
- Beauty may **cost** — the false-gift Cache keeps danger honest.

**Acceptance test (every fused asset):** 1) symbolic verb? 2) state before→after? 3) surface that enforces it? 4) material that remembers the cost? 5) tiny emblem that carries the verdict? 6) generalized beyond any source? 7) drift rejected?
