Gizmo · Lesson 0014 · Solid arena

Enemies respect the pillars

The art stream dressed the flat floor into the Clockwork Observatory — a beacon, pylons, debris. But the Simulation moves enemies on a flat plane with no idea those props exist, so they ghosted straight through. This lesson makes the arena geometry solid to enemies — the gap 0013 flagged as "judge it live."

Why this, now — two movers, two worlds

Your arena has two movers owned by two different worlds, and that seam (ADR 0002) is the lesson. Enemies live in the headless Simulation (the rules-world) and move by flat-plane seek + separation. Gizmo lives in the Godot scene (the physics-world) and moves by CharacterBody3D physics. A prop is only solid to a mover if the obstacle exists in the world that owns that mover. The art stream already gave each prop a StaticBody3D — so Gizmo collided with the beacon all along. The enemies didn't, because nothing put the beacon into the rules-world. This lesson adds that missing half.

Part 1 — the technique is one you already wrote

Back in 0008, _separate_enemies() pushed two overlapping circles apart. An obstacle is the exact same push-out against a static circle. The beacon and pylons are roughly cylindrical, so a circle footprint on the XZ plane is faithful and tiny to write. We stay "soft push-out, not navigation" — precisely what ADR 0002 commits to (no navmesh, no pathfinder):

func _resolve_obstacles() -> void:
    for e in enemies:
        for o in obstacles:
            var delta := e.position - o.position
            delta.y = 0.0
            var d := delta.length()
            var min_d := o.radius + e.radius
            if d < min_d:                       # overlapping the pillar
                e.position += delta / d * (min_d - d)   # eject to the rim

It runs last in _update_enemies() — after seek, separation, and contact knockback — so the post-tick invariant always holds: no enemy ends a frame inside an obstacle, even if an earlier step nudged it in. A nice surprise fell out for free: an off-axis enemy seeking past a pillar doesn't just pin — the redirecting seek plus the radial push-out makes it slide around the pillar to reach you.

Part 2 — opt-in, so balance stays pinned

Obstacles are an empty list by default. That's deliberate: the 0013 balance harness builds a bare Simulation on open floor, so leaving obstacles empty means its 32 pinned checks are untouched — the regression guard stays valid. The live arena is the only thing that adds pillars, via the scene feeding the rules engine (ADR 0002):

class Obstacle:                       # a circular XZ footprint
    var position; var radius

func add_obstacle(pos, radius)        # the scene calls this; the sim stays scene-agnostic

The game_controller wires it with one source of truth and zero scene edits: every world-kit piece already declares a footprint_meters and already carries a collider for Gizmo. So the controller mirrors each solid piece's footprint into the sim — excluding the two walkable roles (walkable_tile, foundation), or the 18×18 foundation would shove every enemy off a 9 m circle.

Part 3 — red first (and a harness gotcha)

The test went red before green — but quietly. Calling a not-yet-existing add_obstacle() doesn't fail a _check; it errors mid-test and the count just doesn't rise (69, not 71). Worth knowing: in this harness a missing method reads as a silent skip, not a red bar — you confirm red by reading the Nonexistent function error, then watch the count climb when it's green.

First mechanic with no simulation.ts source

The Phaser arena is open floor — it has no obstacles. So this is the first rule we've added that isn't a port; it's a deliberate 3D-arena addition, flagged as such in the code. And it's push-out, not pathfinding: an enemy can still pin against a pillar in the degenerate head-on case rather than route cleverly around it. That's enough for v1 cover; if the feel ever needs smarter routing, that's a tangential-slide or navmesh lesson — named, not built now.

Done means…

Done = enemies never end a frame inside arena geometry, proven by run_simulation_tests.gd (71 checks, full suite green: controller 10 · playable-slice 766 · balance still 32) and live — the running scene registered 27 obstacles (beacon + 14 pylons + 12 debris, zero floors), with enemies_inside_obstacle: 0 and a 16-enemy cluster dropped on the beacon all ejected to its rim. You can explain the two-movers-two-worlds seam, why push-out (not a navmesh) fits ADR 0002, and why obstacles are opt-in.
