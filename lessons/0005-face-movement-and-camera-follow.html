Gizmo · Lesson 0005 · The character

Face movement & camera follow

Stop the moonwalk, and let the camera travel with Gizmo — without ever leaving the Diablo angle.

Why this, now

Two small things turn "a model sliding around" into "a character you're piloting." First, Gizmo should turn to face where he's going. Second, the camera should follow him so he never slides off the edge of the floor and out of view. The second one is where the promise from 0002 — fixed angle, movable position — finally cashes out.

Part 1 — Face the direction of travel

Right now Gizmo keeps facing the camera while gliding sideways (the moonwalk). We want his body to rotate so his front points along his velocity. Two ideas do it:

  • atan2 turns a direction into an angle. Given our movement direction on the ground (x, z), atan2(direction.x, direction.z) is the Y-rotation that points the model's front (its +Z face) along that direction.
  • lerp_angle eases from the current angle to the target angle, taking the short way around the circle. That gives a smooth turn instead of an instant snap.

We rotate the Model child, not the body — the physics body stays a clean, axis-aligned frame; only the visual spins. (And note: turning the model does not change how he moves — movement is world-relative, remember, so facing is purely cosmetic.)

Build Part 1 — edit gizmo.gd

Open scripts/gizmo.gd and update it to this (new lines: turn_speed, the model reference, and the two facing lines in the moving branch):

extends CharacterBody3D

## How fast Gizmo moves, in metres per second.
@export var speed: float = 6.0
## How quickly he reaches top speed and how hard he brakes.
@export var acceleration: float = 40.0
@export var friction: float = 50.0
## How quickly he turns to face his travel direction.
@export var turn_speed: float = 12.0

@onready var model: Node3D = $Model

func _physics_process(delta: float) -> void:
	var input := Input.get_vector(&"move_left", &"move_right", &"move_up", &"move_down")
	var direction := Vector3(input.x, 0.0, input.y)
	if direction != Vector3.ZERO:
		velocity.x = move_toward(velocity.x, direction.x * speed, acceleration * delta)
		velocity.z = move_toward(velocity.z, direction.z * speed, acceleration * delta)
		# Face travel direction (model's front is +Z), turning smoothly.
		var target_yaw := atan2(direction.x, direction.z)
		model.rotation.y = lerp_angle(model.rotation.y, target_yaw, turn_speed * delta)
	else:
		velocity.x = move_toward(velocity.x, 0.0, friction * delta)
		velocity.z = move_toward(velocity.z, 0.0, friction * delta)
	move_and_slide()

$Model is the gizmo.glb child you renamed Model in 0004 — that's why this reference works. We only update facing while moving, so he keeps his last heading when he stops (instead of snapping back to forward).

If he faces backward

This assumes the model's front is its +Z side (ours is). If yours runs away from where it's heading, the model's front is −Z instead — fix it by adding half a turn: atan2(direction.x, direction.z) + PI. Good thing to know: model "forward" isn't standardized, so this is a normal one-line adjustment, not a bug in your logic.

Part 2 — The camera follows (still fixed angle)

The camera should keep its exact −50° tilt and never rotate — it should only slide so Gizmo stays roughly centered. That's the whole "fixed angle, movable position" idea: we touch the camera's position every frame and never its rotation.

The trick: keep a constant offset — the camera's spot relative to Gizmo. Right now the camera sits at (0, 12, 10) and Gizmo's at the origin, so the offset is exactly (0, 12, 10). Each frame we aim the camera at gizmo.position + offset, easing in with a lerp for a gentle trailing feel.

Build Part 2 — a camera follow script
  1. Open scenes/main.tscn, select the Camera3D.
  2. Attach Script → res://scripts/camera_rig.gd → Create. Replace with:
extends Camera3D

## The node to follow — assign Gizmo in the Inspector.
@export var target: Node3D
## Camera position relative to the target — preserves the Diablo height/distance.
@export var offset: Vector3 = Vector3(0.0, 12.0, 10.0)
## How quickly the camera catches up (higher = snappier).
@export var follow_speed: float = 8.0

func _process(delta: float) -> void:
	if target == null:
		return
	var desired := target.global_position + offset
	global_position = global_position.lerp(desired, follow_speed * delta)
  1. With the Camera3D still selected, find the new Target field in the Inspector (under the script). Click it → Assign → pick the Gizmo node. (Or drag Gizmo from the Scene dock onto the field.)
  2. Press F5 and move around. The camera trails Gizmo, keeping the angle.

Notice the script never touches rotation — only global_position. That's what keeps the tilt locked while the view travels. We follow in _process (every drawn frame) for smoothness; the lerp with follow_speed gives that slight, pleasant lag as the camera catches up.

How you'll know it worked

Walk Gizmo in a circle: he pivots to face each new direction smoothly (no moonwalk), and the camera glides to keep him near the middle of the screen — always looking down at the same fixed angle, never spinning. Crank follow_speed up for a locked-on feel or down for a lazier drift; bump turn_speed for snappier pivots. They're exported, so tweak them live in the Inspector.
