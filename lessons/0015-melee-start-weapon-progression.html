Gizmo · Lesson 0015 · Weapon progression

The melee start — and why the opening had no fight

Gizmo no longer begins fully armed. He starts with a single rudimentary melee auto-attack — "fending for his life" — and drafts ranged spark, fireball, void, and explosives as he levels. This is a design pivot (ADR 0004), pinned by a probe and a test, with the ranged logic kept intact.

Why this — the opening had nothing to tune

The request was "tune the first level so Gizmo can kill the single trash mob before taking too much damage." But a probe of the current ranged auto-fire told the truth: standing still, Gizmo took zero damage in 30 s — enemies died at 5 m before they could touch him. There was no opening fight to tune. Meanwhile the hero concept art shows Gizmo cradling a spark in its palm — a gentle channeler that grows into power, not one that starts armed. Both point the same way: start rudimentary (melee), earn range through drafts.

Part 1 — the swappable seam pays off

Back in 0013 the combat seam (_update_weapon) was built to be swappable on purpose. That foresight makes this pivot tiny — the melee start is a config change, not a rewrite:

const MELEE_RANGE := 1.6      # rudimentary starting reach
const ATTACK_RANGE := 5.0     # the Spark Chain's reach once DRAFTED (kept, dormant)

var attack_range := MELEE_RANGE   # Gizmo STARTS melee; drafting the Spark Chain sets this to ATTACK_RANGE

The ranged Spark Chain ported in 0009/0013 isn't scrapped — it just moves from "the starting weapon" to "the first draftable upgrade." Fireball / void / explosives join it as later drafts. The auto-attack stays auto-targeted the whole time (Megabonk / Brotato style); only the kit grows.

Part 2 — what a short reach changes (the whole loop)

Reach is the dial that flips the genre. At 5 m it's a keep-away game; at 1.6 m it's a close-in game. The probe makes the new opening concrete:

single trash mob (contact live)   killed at 4.9s,  HP lost = 0    -> clean kill, the asked-for beat
standing still                    dies ~40s                      -> movement now matters from level 1
light movement                    survives 30s+ on chip damage

A lone mob is a clean kill because melee reaches it (2.6 m) just before it can touch (1.4 m) — a ~0.5 s window the auto-attack lands. Pile on a second mob and that window tightens: that's the "fending for his life" tension. And standing still is finally lethal — with no range to farm from, you have to reposition.

Part 3 — the honest split (don't fake-pass the late game)

Melee is the only weapon for the whole run right now — and melee-only can't clear a late swarm. So full-run balance is intentionally deferred until the draft system exists. The harness is split to say exactly that, instead of loosening bands to go green:

NEW  melee-start opening   single-mob clean kill · standing-still lethal in 20–45s   (default config)
KEEP 0013 full-run curve   stationary/kite/TTK/pressure  — pinned at ATTACK_RANGE     (the DRAFTABLE ranged weapon)
NOT  full-run melee-only   not asserted — unfair until drafts land, and we say so

The 0013 profiles now set attack_range = ATTACK_RANGE explicitly, so they keep guarding the ranged Spark Chain's curve — the weapon is retained and still tested. Balance harness: PASS 38 (was 32; +6 melee checks). Full suite green: sim 71 · controller 10 · playable-slice 766.

Prototype — the draft system is now on the critical path

This makes the opening feel right and the mid/late run thin out (melee-only). That's expected: "choose an upgrade on level-up" (the Core Matrix) just graduated from a deferred nicety to the thing that makes a full run fair. The prototype Spark-Chain auto-scale from 0013 stays as a temporary bridge until real draft choices replace it.

Done means…

Done = Gizmo starts at melee reach (1.6 m); a single opening trash mob is a clean kill (0 HP lost, contact live); standing still naturally loses in the opening window; and the retained ranged Spark Chain is still pinned by the 0013 profiles. Verify: godot --headless --path godot --script res://tests/run_balance_tests.gd → PASS 38. You can explain why the ranged opening had no fight, why a short reach inverts the loop, and why full-run balance is deferred to the draft system rather than faked green.

Deferred — named, not built

The Core Matrix draft system (level-up → choose ranged spark / fireball / void / explosives), then a full-run rebalance with drafts in play. Animation for a visible melee swing (the rig has no clips yet) is a later polish lesson, not a v1 blocker.
