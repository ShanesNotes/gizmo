# Cinematic grammar — what makes Elden Ring / Hades openings land (lore lane, 2026-07-07 night)

One page, applied to every cinematic beat the lore lane ships tonight. Research beat
distilled from the two references the mission names; craft observations, not lore canon.

## The five laws

1. **Voice before image.** Elden Ring's opening speaks over near-darkness and still
   frames; the narrator earns the player's ear before the eye has anything to judge.
   Hades gives you Zagreus' voice within seconds of control. → Margin speaks out of
   ember-dark before her portrait exists. Never show, then explain; address, then reveal.

2. **Image cadence is liturgical, not montage.** Elden Ring's prologue holds each
   tableau 4–8 seconds, cuts on the narrator's clause boundaries, and repeats a
   compositional motif (a figure small against a vast broken thing). The cut lands
   where the sentence lands. → Every opening beat's min-time is tied to its VO clause;
   camera moves are slow pushes/settles, one per beat, never during a cut.

3. **Name the problem, then name the player.** Both openings state the world's wound
   first (the Shattering; the house of the dead) and only then the player's relation to
   it ("Arise now, ye Tarnished"; Zagreus' escape). The player is addressed as a role
   with a duty, in second person, exactly once. → the light failed → *you* keep it.
   "Little keeper" is our "ye Tarnished" — it appears once in the opening, at the turn.

4. **The title card is the downbeat, not the start.** Elden Ring holds the title until
   the prologue's thesis has landed, on the loudest musical swell, then silence after.
   Hades stamps its title on the first escape attempt, mid-momentum. → GIZMO's title
   card lands after "keep it alive," on the swell sting's peak, held in silence for a
   beat before the controls plate. Swell → title → silence is one unit; never title
   over speech.

5. **Teaching is diegetic and last.** Neither opening pauses for a tutorial screen
   before meaning is established; controls arrive inside the fiction (Hades: the first
   chamber; ER: the tutorial cave is skippable and framed as a fall). → the brass
   controls plate stays the final movement, framed as Margin's marginalia, and the
   sequence stays skippable at any instant with no penalty.

## Music grammar (cinematic cues only — run score is the soundtrack lab's)

- One swell per cinematic; it crests **on the title card**, not on dialogue.
- Under VO: bed stays below speech (the VoiceReserved duck is law); no melodic
  competition with Margin's register.
- End on release, not resolution — the run door should feel like the held breath.

## Rejects (gate: a beat violating these is recut, not shipped)

- Title card over active VO. — law 4
- Two camera moves in one beat, or a move across a cut. — law 2
- Second-person address more than once before the portrait reveal. — law 3
- Any unskippable segment, or skip that leaks VO into the hub. — law 5
- Exposition that a threshold line could carry later (save it for the threshold). — moat law
