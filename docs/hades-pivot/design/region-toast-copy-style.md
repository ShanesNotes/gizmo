# Region-toast copy style — note from the lore pass (2026-07-06)

For the agent wiring region-entry toasts. Do not treat this as canon; canon lives in
`/home/ark/gizmo-lore/canon/` (`copy-rules.yaml`, `world-structure.md`). This is the
applied style so toast copy lands inside the fence on the first try.

## Shape
- One line: region name, optionally followed by a short epithet.
  `Hearthwake Basin` or `Hearthwake Basin — the workshop remembers.`
- Sentence case for the epithet; no exclamation marks; no second sentence.
- The toast states *where you are*, never *what to do* — objective copy is owned by
  `copy-rules.yaml` `runtime_objectives` ("Reach the Beacon", "Rekindle the Beacon").

## Voice
Warm, earnest, ceremonial, concise, sad-but-savable. Pressure is weather, not warfare:
"the cold closes in", "the swarm gathers" — never military callouts.

## Hard rejects (gates from canon)
- No wave/round/countdown/stage/chapter words, and no counters ("Depth 3", "Room 2/8").
- No "Spark of Humanity" in toasts (premise term, ceremony only); Sparks = currency.
- No saint names, no real platform/company names, ever.
- Region names beyond Hearthwake (Hearthless Rows, Glare Shoals, Colloquy, Fold) are
  draft in the lore canon — usable, but expect them to be reconciled against the region
  graph; keep them data-driven, not hardcoded in logic.
