# 06 — Map & Environment (imagegen 2.0 plates → Meshy kit dressing)

`derived from ecosystem canon; do not edit as source`

Spatial law (`gizmo-level-design/canon/`): one directed spine, warm south → cold north;
exposure rises to a unique Beacon peak with a mandatory sanctuary trough just before it; flat
walkable layer, all verticality non-walkable scenery; silhouette hierarchy tall+warm-emissive =
goal, short+cool = obstacle; thresholds are walk-through frames at real crossings; camera band
shows ~20–26 ground units, so distant goals need staged "pull-target" glimpses. Every plate
must state its **pole** and read in flat-shaded silhouette before painting (X-S4). Pole flips
exactly twice on the spine: living→hollowed at trial, hollowed→living at Rekindled (X-S2).
Drain-deep `#2c3633` appears ONLY at the Beacon peak.

These plates are mood/dressing witnesses for the kit assembly (q01–q14) and lighting design —
never authority over the promoted zone graph.

### MAP-00 · island master plate (top-down-ish 3/4 overview)
> A hand-painted gouache overview of one wounded floating island seen from a high 3/4 angle
> against a deep violet-indigo void with warm nebula smears: at the south edge, a warm hearth
> alcove glowing gold inside a broken U of wall with one clear northward gap; a flat carved-
> sandstone spine running north, splitting around a low ridge into an eastern gear-alcove arm
> and a western scrap-alcove arm that rejoin at a circular gear-henge plaza with a tall
> heart-spire; beyond it an open trial ground ringed by freestanding cover pillars; then, off
> the spine to the east, a small sheltered sanctuary dome breathing warm light; and at the far
> cold north tip, a tall grey dormant hearth-beacon on a dais, the island's darkest, most
> drained ground. Color temperature grades continuously warm-south to cold-north; one unbroken
> gold thread of path-light runs hearth to beacon. The beacon is NOT visible from the hearth —
> a ridge and the henge stage its reveal.
- Provenance: spec §2/§4, pressure-zones curve rules, X-L2 thread, M2 composition.

### MAP-01 · Hearth / SouthLandingZone (pole: living-faced, exposure floor)
> Interior of the warm hearth alcove at dawn-light: carved sandstone walls in a sheltering U,
> a bronze fire-shelf with a small kept flame, soot-shadow of years of tending, worn tool
> racks, a floor polished by small feet, and one bright gap in the wall opening north where
> the cold road begins — the gap framed so leaving reads as the only and obvious choice.
> Warmth behind the viewer, cold ahead.

### MAP-02 · Threshold bridge (first crossing)
> A flat sandstone-and-bronze footbridge leaving the hearth's light, passing under a
> freestanding gear-arch threshold with a small unlit keystone lantern; beyond the arch the
> ground's warm tint measurably drains one step toward slate. Crossing reads as a decision
> made with the feet.

### MAP-03 · East gear alcove — Brasswind (branch arm, medium exposure)
> A tighter eastern path threading standing bronze observatory instruments — tilted gear
> rings, a cracked astrolabe pillar — warm brass tones still holding but dust-veiled; a
> glimpse of violet spark-crystals in a niche promises reward; the far mouth rejoins the
> spine. Reads riskier than the hearth, kinder than the west.

### MAP-04 · West scrap alcove — Rustchain (branch arm, medium-hot exposure)
> A broader, colder western path through toppled salvage: drained bronze plate-stacks, hanging
> chains, a scrap-cluster mound, longer sightlines with fewer refuges; grey-green drain
> visibly further along here, first blank-faced debris shapes among the wreckage. Richer
> salvage, thinner comfort — a real choice against the east, not a coin flip (L5).

### MAP-05 · Gear-henge plaza / CentralGearPlazaZone (landmark, pole: living but strained)
> A wide circular workshop basin — an open standable plaza, not a corridor — ringed by the
> monumental broken gear-henge arc, the tall heart-spire rising at its center as a
> circumnavigable island; carved star-notation on the stones; the spire's lantern niche dark
> but intact. From its northern rim, the first staged glimpse of the distant Beacon crown
> enters the frame — a pull-target, far and grey. Tall + faintly warm = the goal register.

### MAP-06 · Trial ground (pole flip #1: living→hollowed; high exposure)
> An open arena pocket of bare drained sandstone, ringed by freestanding broken pillars as
> cover islands — no lane, no wall to hide behind, at least three open approaches; the ground
> here fully in the drain register (`#4a554f`), stamped repeating fracture patterns in the
> paving where hand-worn asymmetry used to be; blank-faced debris watching from the edges.
> The island's forms de-faced and mechanically repeated — nothing sci-fi, nothing gory.

### MAP-07 · Sanctuary approach & interior (relief trough; pole: living-faced island in cold)
> Off the spine eastward, a short sheltered descent to the sanctuary half-dome: warm lamplight
> spilling from its one threshold arch into the surrounding drain, living dry vines with small
> rosettes on the ribs, Wick's kept lamp steady at center; inside, the mix of the world warms —
> repaired lamps, folded cloth, breath. The one warm-lit interior on the northern half; relief
> that never claims safety-forever. A loopback path returns toward the henge.

### MAP-08 · Beacon dais / NorthBeaconDaisZone — Dormant (peak exposure; drain-deep floor)
> The cold north tip: a wide dais of drained stone (`#2c3633`, the only place this deep drain
> appears) holding the great dormant hearth-beacon — grey, ash-veiled, candle-cups snuffed,
> its carved face cracked and cobwebbed; edge-backed hold ground with two to four open ingress
> lanes and no safe corner; the void close on three sides; hanging chains and dead lamps.
> The saddest, stillest place on the island — arrested keeping, dust that never falls.

### MAP-09 · Beacon — Rekindling under siege (climax plate)
> The same dais mid-rekindling: the beacon's vessel catching — warm ember flame `#e0461e`
> growing, the tiny teal core-spark alive at its heart (the only teal in the plate besides
> Gizmo's eye), warmth fighting outward against the drain while hollow household machines
> press in through the ingress lanes at peak density; crimson only where blows land. Gizmo
> small and unbroken at the center of the hold. Effort, not victory yet.

### MAP-10 · Beacon — Rekindled / the road opens (pole flip #2: hollowed→living)
> The dais transformed: the beacon crowned with an open warm gold sun-face mandorla, warmth
> visibly rolling south across the ground it had lost — drained stone re-warming in a gradient
> away from the crown, candle-cups lit, cobwebs gone to light; far beyond the island's north
> edge, faint further islands of the Shattered Meridian catching the first thrown light — a
> narrative horizon only, no walkable path implied. Quiet light, not fireworks.

### MAP-11 · Horizon omen skyline (non-walkable backdrop)
> A distant skyline band for the island's void horizon: broken silhouettes of farther
> Meridian islands and dead beacon-spires in deep indigo and night-blue, one crow-silhouette
> perched on the nearest broken arch (a single omen at a charged threshold, M7); painterly,
> hazeless at the band itself, clearly unreachable scenery.

## Lighting/dressing law for kit assembly (for Codex when placing q01–q14)

Exposure gradient is expressed by material/lighting register, not labels: warm hearth ground →
neutral shoulder → cold approach → drained wilds; off-path ground zoned so unclaimed space
reads as world, not void (negative-space rule). Landmarks must read by silhouette + lighting at
the fixed camera before any painterly pass (greybox lighting probe first — X-S4, G12: the
gouache shader is a target, not an assumption). No walkable geometry may occlude Gizmo. No two
high-exposure areas adjacent without relief between. Sanctuary trough immediately precedes the
Beacon peak, always.
